Featured Project · Multi-Tenant Booking Platform

Booking Engine

A booking and scheduling platform built to host multiple service businesses, not just one: salons, barbershops, consultants, anyone stuck taking bookings over the phone or through WhatsApp back-and-forth. Each business gets its own branded booking page; clients pick a service, day, and time slot, and the business gets a clean queue with no double-bookings. Zed-Forge is the first business live on the platform, showing the full flow end-to-end.

Multi-tenant architecture: one platform, independent booking pages per business Service selection with live pricing per option Day and time-slot picker: clients book their own place in line Separate business login to manage bookings per tenant Zed-Forge live as the first demo tenant on the platform
How I Approached It
Problem: Booking over phone or WhatsApp back-and-forth doesn't scale. A business owner juggling messages risks double-booking the same slot, and building a dedicated booking system per client from scratch isn't sustainable to offer affordably.
Decision: Built one multi-tenant platform instead of one-off booking pages. Each business gets an isolated, branded booking page and login, sharing the same underlying slot-locking logic underneath.
Result: Zed-Forge, live as the first tenant, shows a new business can be onboarded as a config change, not a rebuild, without risking the double-booking problem the platform exists to solve.

Featured Project

Evelyn Hone Market

A fully functional campus marketplace for Evelyn Hone College of Applied Arts and Commerce in Lusaka. Students and staff can list items for sale with photos and prices, browse products, and contact sellers directly via WhatsApp or phone. Built with a full user account system, seller profiles, and listing management.

User registration, login & seller profiles Product listings with images & pricing WhatsApp & call integration for buyers Full listing management dashboard Mobile responsive: works on any phone
How I Approached It
Problem: The admin panel was originally protected by a single hardcoded static key shared across the app, not a real access-control model, and a real risk if it ever leaked.
Decision: Replaced it with proper JWT-based server-side authentication, added a dedicated admin flag to the user model, and wrote a migration to roll it out without breaking existing accounts.
Result: Admin access is now tied to an authenticated user role instead of a shared secret, a real security gap closed before the platform saw wider use across campus.
